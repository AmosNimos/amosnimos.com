Introduction:

Canada's recycling system is broken. Despite our best efforts to sort waste, much of it ends up buried or recycled in environmentally harmful ways. It's time to become independent and adopt DIY recycling methods. This article will guide you through the process of home recycling, from understanding types of plastics to creating new products.

Understanding Plastics:

Different types of plastics have different recycling capabilities. Hereâ€™s a quick guide:

  • PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate): Commonly found in beverage bottles. Recyclable into textiles, furniture, and more.
  • HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene): Used in milk jugs, detergent bottles. Recyclable into pipes, plastic lumber, etc.
  • PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride): Found in plumbing pipes, credit cards. Less commonly recycled, can release harmful chemicals.
  • LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene): Used in grocery bags, some food wraps. Recyclable into trash can liners, floor tiles.
  • PP (Polypropylene): Found in yogurt containers, bottle caps. Recyclable into signal lights, battery cables.
  • PS (Polystyrene): Used in disposable coffee cups, plastic food boxes. Difficult to recycle, often ends up in landfills.
  • Other: Includes various plastics like acrylic, nylon. Recycling depends on the specific type and facilities available.

DIY Recycling Process:

Note: This is not a complete step-by-step tutorial, but rather a spark of an idea, a new way of seeing things, and a push in the right direction. For detailed guidance, you can check out channels like Brothers Make, which have very good videos on the topic.

Collecting and Sorting:

  • Collect plastics, sort them by type.
  • Clean and disinfect the plastics thoroughly.

Shredding:

  • Shred plastics to reduce storage space and prepare for melting. This can be done using a home shredder or manually.

Melting:

  • Use repurposed appliances like old ovens, grills, or microwaves dedicated to recycling (not for cooking food).
  • Melt plastics in a well-ventilated area to avoid harmful fumes.

Molding:

  • Create molds out of wood or metal for your recycled plastic products.
  • Pour melted plastic into molds and let it cool.

Pressing:

  • Use a DIY press to ensure the plastic takes the shape of the mold.
  • A simple press can be made using a car jack and a sturdy frame.

Creative Uses for Recycled Plastics:

Furniture:

  • Make benches, seats, and tables from melted plastic.
  • Recycled plastics are durable and weather-resistant, perfect for outdoor furniture.

Building Materials:

  • Create pillars, floor tiles, and wall panels.
  • These materials can be used in construction, reducing the need for expensive wood and other materials.

Artistic Projects:

  • Use recycled plastics for art and craft projects.
  • From sculptures to decorative items, the possibilities are endless.

Safety Considerations:

Health Risks:

  • Always melt plastics in a separate appliance from those used for cooking to avoid microplastics contamination.
  • Ensure good ventilation to minimize exposure to harmful fumes.

Proper Usage:

  • Avoid using recycled plastics for food containers or cookware due to potential chemical leaching.
  • Be mindful of the types of plastics you use and the products you create.

Conclusion:

DIY recycling empowers us to take control of our waste management. By understanding different plastics and creatively repurposing them, we can reduce our reliance on flawed government systems and make a tangible impact on the environment. With a bit of creativity and effort, we can turn trash into treasure and contribute to a more sustainable future.

Disclaimer:

Recycling plastics at home should be done with caution. Proper ventilation and dedicated equipment are essential to avoid health risks. Always research the types of plastics you use and ensure safe practices to protect yourself and the environment.
